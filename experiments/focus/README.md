# Focus Experiment 🦍

An interactive browser-based awareness game inspired by the famous **Selective Attention Test** by Simons & Chabris (1999) – commonly known as the "Invisible Gorilla Experiment."

## What it does

Players are asked to catch as many pizzas as possible in a busy restaurant scene. While focused on the task, most players completely miss a gorilla walking through the background – demonstrating how the human brain actively filters out information that isn't deemed relevant in the moment.

This phenomenon is known as **Inattentional Blindness** and is closely linked to the brain's **Reticular Activating System (RAS)**.

## Who it's for

Designed for workshops, coaching sessions, and educational settings with participants aged 17–29. Works equally well with adults of any age.

## Background

> *"We perceived only what we were looking for, and failed to see what we weren't."*
> – Simons & Chabris, 1999

The original experiment showed that when people are asked to count basketball passes, around **50% fail to notice a person in a gorilla suit** walking through the scene. This experiment replicates that effect in an engaging, interactive format.
